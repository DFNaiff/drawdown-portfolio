\begin{abstract}
Objetive:
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
& \text{maximize} \\
& \quad x_T^T \alpha \\
& \text{s.t.} \\
& \quad \alpha \in \Delta_N \\
& \quad (1-\gamma) \max_{s \in {0,\ldots,t}}{x_s^T \alpha} - x_t^T \alpha \leq 0, \forall t. \\
\end{split}
\end{equation}
Here, $\gamma$ is the drawdown tolerance, $x_t$ the portfolio price vector at $t$, and $\alpha$ is the asset.

If we define $m_t$ as an "envelope" for the series maximum, then minimizing the following problem is equivalent to minimize the original problem:
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
     & \text{minimize} \\ 
     & \quad (-\lambda_1 x_T,\lambda_2   \mathbf{1}_{N})^T (\alpha, m) \\
     & \text{s.t.} \\
     & \quad -\alpha \leq 0 \\
     & \quad (1-\gamma) m_t - x_t^T \alpha \leq 0, \quad t=1, \ldots, N \\
     & \quad m_t - m_{t+1} \leq 0, \quad t=1, \ldots, N-1 \\
     & \quad x_t^T \alpha - m_t \leq 0, \quad t=2, \ldots, T \\
     & \quad \mathbf{1}^T \alpha - 1 = 0 \\
     & \quad m_1 - x_1^T \alpha = 0
\end{split}
\end{equation}
\end{abstract}
